HG-SCRUB : Human Gaussian Single Camera Relighting with Unified Blending

Junsuk (John) Kim1, Neil Gautam1,
1Texas A&M University - College Station

Problem summary

The task addressed in this project is the realistic relighting and blending of human avatars captured from monocular video into novel target scenes. While recent advances in 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) and NeRF have enabled high-fidelity visual reconstructions, these methods often fall short in producing physically consistent integration—especially under novel lighting conditions or when ensuring contact realism between avatars and scene surfaces. Most prior systems depend on multi-camera setups or lack unified handling of both relighting and blending. Thus, the goal is to develop a pipeline that achieves both relighting and seamless integration using only monocular input, lowering the barrier to entry for avatar-based AR/VR and content generation.

[Previous work]

HUGS: Demonstrated the use of 3DGS for human reconstruction but lacked scene-aware relighting and alignment.
  • The Relightables: High-quality multi-camera relighting system, not scalable to general users.
  • PhysAvatar: Modeled physically plausible interactions but depended on detailed observations.
  • GS-IR: Provided a foundation for inverse rendering using 3D Gaussians (normals, albedo, roughness).
  • DiffusionLight: Enabled generation of environment maps via chrome ball images.
  • BiGS: Addressed relightable Gaussian primitives.
  • SuGaR: Focused on aligning Gaussian geometry to mesh surfaces—an inspiration for improving avatar placement.

    [Description of work]

    Constructed a full pipeline accepting monocular video and a target scene image.
  • Reconstructed 3D human Gaussian splats in both canonical and posed space using LBS (Linear Blend Skinning).
  • Applied GS-IR and DiffusionLight to extract and learn environment lighting and material maps.
  • Extracted {Normal, Albedo, Roughness, Metallic} from both human and target scenes.
  • Performed early-stage relighting using both learned environment maps and external chromeball references.
  • Developed initial routines for unifying the human and scene Gaussians with matched geometry and materials.

  • [Results]

    Achieved relit human Gaussians that respond to target scene lighting.
  • Successfully decomposed both the human and scene into their physical components (N, A, R, M).
  • Performed visual relighting with DiffusionLight and GS-IR.
  • Preliminary blended renderings show promise but require refinement.
  • Conducted analysis of normal maps for both canonical and posed human reconstructions.
  • Achieved 3DGS training up to 15,000 iterations, showing convergence in both human and scene representations.

  • [Analysis of work]

    New Results Demonstrated a single-camera pipeline for scene-aware avatar relighting and early blending—previously only possible with multi-camera setups. Integrated GS-IR with 3D Gaussian Splatting for both human and environment representations. Used Diffusion-based light probes for real-time environment estimation—this cross-method integration is novel. Meeting Goals Partially met: Achieved strong relighting, but blending is still a WIP. Did not yet fully implement human-scene contact constraints (floating artifacts remain). Geometry-aware alignment needs improvement to match SMPL surfaces tightly with scene surfaces.

    [Future Work]

    Short-term Finalize unified blending of the human Gaussian with the scene Gaussian. Improve SMPL-guided contact fidelity to reduce float artifacts. Fine-tune material and lighting extraction with more stable inverse rendering techniques. Long-term Introduce temporal consistency constraints across video frames. Generalize pipeline to moving backgrounds or outdoor scenes. Explore real-time adaptation using lightweight models for AR applications.
